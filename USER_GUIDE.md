## Tab 1: Campaign Command Center

### What You See
A table showing each active campaign with:
- **Campaign Name**
- **Platform** (Meta or Google)
- **Total Spend** (lifetime or this period)
- **Leads Generated** (or conversions)
- **Cost Per Lead (CPL)** (lower is better)
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** (percent of impressions that became clicks)
- **Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)** (if conversion value is tracked)

### Using the Campaign Tab

**View Campaign List**
1. Open MERIDIAN → click the **Campaign Command Center** tab
2. See all your campaigns listed
3. Sort by any column (click the column header)

**Drill Down Into a Campaign**
1. Click any campaign name
2. You'll see:
   - **Daily breakdown:** How much you spent each day, how many leads per day
   - **Audience targeting:** Who you're reaching (age, location, interests, etc.)
   - **Creative rotation:** Which ads are running and their individual performance
   - **Performance trends:** Charts showing spend vs. leads over time

Example: You click on "Q1 2026 Lead Gen Campaign"  
→ You see a calendar view showing: Day 1: $500 spend, 23 leads | Day 2: $487 spend, 19 leads | etc.  
→ You spot that Day 5–7 had lower leads despite same spend → might indicate audience fatigue

**Why Drill-Down Matters**
Instead of seeing only the big picture ("Campaign spent $5,000, got 150 leads"), you can see exactly:
- Which day the campaign went flat
- Which audience segment converts best
- Which creative ad has the lowest CPL
- When to pause underperforming ads

### Setting Up Campaign Connections

**Before campaigns appear, you need to authorize MERIDIAN to see your data.**

#### Step 1: Connect Your Meta Ads Account

1. Go to MERIDIAN → Campaign Command Center tab
2. Look for the button: **"Connect Meta Ads"**
3. Click it → You'll be redirected to Facebook
4. Log in with your Facebook account (the one that manages your ad campaigns)
5. You'll see: "_MERIDIAN_ wants permission to access your Ad Accounts"
6. Click **Approve**
7. You'll be sent back to MERIDIAN
8. After a few seconds, your campaigns will appear

**What's Happening**
- MERIDIAN is asking Facebook for permission to VIEW your campaigns and spending
- You'll never give Von or anyone else your Facebook password
- You're just giving MERIDIAN the right to read (not change) your campaigns
- You can revoke this permission anytime in Facebook Settings → Apps and Websites

#### Step 2: Connect Your Google Ads Account

1. In MERIDIAN → Campaign Command Center tab
2. Look for the button: **"Connect Google Ads"**
3. Click it → You'll be redirected to Google
4. Log in with your Google account (the one that manages your Google Ads)
5. Click **Approve** when asked for permission
6. You'll be sent back to MERIDIAN
7. Google campaigns will appear

**Troubleshooting Campaign Setup**

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| "I don't see a Connect button" | Refresh the page. If still missing, let Von know. |
| "Red error saying 'Invalid Account'" | You need admin access to the ad account. In Meta Ads Manager or Google Ads, make sure you're an Admin, not just a User. |
| "Campaigns appear but spend numbers look wrong" | MERIDIAN updates every 5 minutes. Wait a few minutes for data to sync. If still wrong after 15 min, let Von know. |
| "I see only some of my campaigns" | You might have multiple ad accounts. Check both Meta and Google to see if all accounts are authorized. |

### Using Campaign Data (Examples)

**Weekly Budget Review**
- Every Monday, open MERIDIAN → Campaign tab
- Glance at spend and leads for the week so far
- Spot any campaigns overspending or underperforming
- Text Von if something looks off

**Monthly Performance Report**
- Drill into each campaign → see the daily breakdown
- Note the best and worst performing days
- Compare CPL or ROAS across campaigns
- Use this data for your monthly board report or donor update

**Reallocating Budget**
- You see Campaign A (CPL $50) is outperforming Campaign B (CPL $85)

- Text Von: "Let's test reallocating $1,000 from B to A for next 48 hours"
- Check back in 48 hours → see if CPL improved

## Tab 2: CRM & Pipeline

### What You See
A kanban-style board showing contacts organized by stage:
- **New** — Leads you just acquired
- **Qualified** — Good fit, interest confirmed
- **Proposal** — You've sent a proposal
- **Negotiation** — Back-and-forth, getting closer
- **Closed** — Deal won or lost

### Adding a Contact

1. Open MERIDIAN → CRM & Pipeline tab
2. Click **"+ Add Contact"** button
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Name** (required)
   - **Email**
   - **Phone**
   - **Company**
   - **Title**
   - **Tags** (e.g., "nonprofit-cfo", "major-donor", "hot-prospect")
   - **Notes** (e.g., "Met at March gala, interested in FY27 sponsorship")
   - **Social Links** (LinkedIn URL, Twitter, etc.)
   - **Headshot** (upload a photo)

4. Pick a **Stage** (New, Qualified, Proposal, Negotiation, Closed)
5. Click **Save**

### Moving a Contact Between Stages

**You can drag and drop:**
1. Find contact on the board (e.g., in "Qualified")
2. Click and drag them to a new stage (e.g., drag to "Proposal")
3. That's it — the move is saved automatically

**Or edit directly:**
1. Click on a contact card
2. Change the stage dropdown
3. Click **Save**

### Updating Contact Info

1. Click on a contact name to open their full profile
2. Edit any field:
   - Add/update phone number
   - Add/update email
   - Upload or change headshot
   - Add/change tags
   - Add notes

3. Click **Save** (or it saves automatically)

### Using the CRM for Follow-Ups

**See when you last talked to someone:**
1. Each contact shows "Last Contact: [date]"
2. If it says "Last Contact: 45 days ago" → that's your signal to reach out

**Using Tags for Priorities:**
1. Tag contacts e.g., "needs-call-this-week" or "waiting-for-them"
2. Filter by tag to see all tagged contacts at once
3. Work through them systematically

**Adding Notes Over Time:**
1. Every time you talk to a contact, add a note:
   - "Called 3/20 — interested in brand partnership, send deck"
   - "Email 3/23 — waiting for budget approval from their CFO"
   - "Met at gala 3/25 — great energy, potential major donor"
2. Next time you open their card, you have full context

### Uploading a Headshot Photo

1. Click on a contact → scroll to **Headshot** section
2. Click **"Upload Photo"**
3. Choose a JPG or PNG from your computer
4. Photo uploads and is saved

**Tips:**
- Square or close-to-square photos work best (not super wide or tall)
- Headshots work better than full-body photos
- Keep file under 5MB for fastest upload

## Tab 3: Nonprofit Finance

### What You See
Three main sections:

**1. Donor Pipeline**
A visual funnel showing:
- Education: Prospects who know about you
- Engagement: They've engaged (attended event, opened emails, etc.)
- Ask: You've made a funding request
- Thank: Donors who've given

**2. Donor Records & Give History**
Individual donor profiles with gift history, preferences, capacity notes.

**3. Grant Tracker**
List of grants you've applied for or are pursuing, with deadlines, award amounts, and status.

**4. YTD Totals**
Summary showing: Where did our money come from this year?

### Tracking Donors

#### Adding a Donor

1. Open MERIDIAN → Nonprofit Finance tab
2. Click **"+ Add Donor"**
3. Fill in:
   - **Name**
   - **Amount Given** (most recent gift)
   - **Date of Gift**
   - **Capacity Estimate** (guess at how much they could give: $1–5K, $5–25K, $25K+)
   - **Preferences** (e.g., education programs, capital projects)
   - **Notes** (e.g., "Met at 2024 gala, gave $2,500 for college scholarship fund")
4. Click **Save**

#### Moving a Donor Through the Pipeline

The pipeline shows donor journey: Education → Engagement → Ask → Thank

1. In the Donor Pipeline section, find the donor
2. Drag them to the next stage (or click edit to change stage)
3. Example:
   - Move from "Education" to "Engagement" once they RSVP to an event
   - Move to "Ask" once you've made your pitch
   - Move to "Thank" after they've contributed

**Why This Matters**
As donors move through the pipeline, you see:
- How many prospects you have at each stage
- Conversion rates (of 10 in Education, how many become donors?)
- Where you need to focus (maybe you have lots of people in Engagement but few moving to Ask)

### Tracking Grants

#### Adding a Grant

1. Go to MERIDIAN → Nonprofit Finance tab
2. Click **"+ Add Grant"**
3. Fill in:
   - **Grant Name** (e.g., "CDC Summer Youth Program")
   - **Funder** (e.g., "Gates Foundation")
   - **Amount** ($250,000)
   - **Deadline** (date)
   - **Status** (Applied, Awarded, Pending, Rejected)
   - **Notes** (e.g., "Summer 2026 cohort, submitted 2/15")
4. Click **Save**

#### Updating Grant Status

1. Find grant in the list under **Grant Tracker**
2. Click to edit
3. Change **Status** dropdown (Applied → Awarded once you hear back)
4. Add note about outcome
5. Click **Save**

### Your YTD Fundraising Overview

**What the Dashboard Shows**
- Total gifts received this year (by month)
- Number of donors
- Average gift size
- Grant awards received

**Drill Down Example:**
- Dashboard shows: "$127,000 raised in March"
- Click on March → see breakdown:
  - $25,000 gift from Alice Chen

  - $60,000 awarded from May Grant (finalized early)
  - Plus several smaller gifts

This drill-down lets you:
- See exactly which donors/grants contributed to your monthly total
- Understand if a big month was driven by one major gift or many smaller ones
- Know which donors to send personalized thank-yous to

## Common Questions & Troubleshooting

### "I lost all my data."
**Cause:** Cleared your browser cache or switched browsers/devices.  
**Solution:** Always use the same browser on the same device. (Until Phase 2.)  
**Temporary help:** If you have a backup, ask Von to help restore.

### "Campaign numbers look wrong."
**Cause:** Data hasn't synced yet (MERIDIAN updates every 5 minutes).  
**Solution:** Wait 10 minutes and refresh. If still wrong, check the original platform (Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads) to confirm. Let Von know if there's a persistent mismatch.

### "When I click a contact, nothing happens."
**Cause:** Contact might be corrupted in localStorage, or browser needs refresh.  
**Solution:** Refresh the page. If contact still won't open, let Von know the contact name.

### "I can't upload a headshot."
**Cause:** File too large or unsupported format (need JPG/PNG).  
**Solutions:**  
- Compress the image first (use Preview on Mac or similar tool)
- Make sure it's JPG or PNG, not WEBP or GIF
- Try a smaller file (< 5MB)

### "My grant deadline is coming up but I wasn't reminded."
**Cause:** Auto-reminders aren't built yet (Phase 2 feature).  
**Solution:** Check your Grant Tracker monthly. Set a phone reminder.

### "Do I need to keep anything backed up?"
**Cause:** Phase 1 only stores data on your device.  
**Solutions:**  
- Export important data (contacts list, campaign data) to CSV periodically
- Take screenshots of YTD totals
- Tell Von about critical data you want backed up
- Phase 2 will have automatic backups

### "Can I use MERIDIAN on my iPhone?"
**Answer:** Yes, the interface is mobile-friendly. But remember: data on iPhone won't sync to Mac until Phase 2. If you need data across devices now, use Phase 1 on your main computer.

## Glossary

**API:** Application Programming Interface. A secure way for apps to talk to each other. (MERIDIAN uses APIs to get data from Meta, Google, your bank, etc.)

**Conversion:** When someone completes a desired action (e.g., submits a form, makes a donation, signs up for an event).

**CPL:** Cost Per Lead. How much you're paying for each lead. Lower is better. (Calculated: Total Spend ÷ Total Leads)

**CPA:** Cost Per Acquisition. How much you're paying per actual customer or donor. (More precise than CPL.)

**CTR:** Click-Through Rate. Percentage of people who saw your ad and clicked it. (Higher is better.)

**ROAS:** Return on Ad Spend. For every $1 spent on ads, how much revenue did you get back? (ROAS 3:1 means $3 back per $1 spent.)

**Kanban:** A board showing tasks/items in stages (like your CRM pipeline: New → Qualified → Proposal → etc.)

**localStorage:** Data stored on your device (your computer/phone). Not on the internet until Phase 2.

**OAuth:** Secure login standard. Lets you authorize MERIDIAN to see your data without giving MERIDIAN your password.

**Drill-Down:** Click a summary to see detailed breakdown. (Click "Campaign A" to see daily breakdown.)
